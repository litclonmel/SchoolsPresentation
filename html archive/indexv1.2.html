LIT Limerick School of Art & Design


  • Limerick School of Art & Design (LSAD) is one of three Faculties in Limerick Institute of Technology (LIT)
  • The largest Art and Design School outside of Dublin (over 1100 students).
  • LSAD traces its roots back to 1852.
  • Outstanding international reputation and also ranked among the top 50 fashion schools in the world.

LSAD at LIT Clonmel


  • The LIT Clonmel Campus is LSAD’s key location for Creative and Interactive Digital Media.
  • Home of the Creative Media & Design, Digital Animation Production and Game Art & Design degree programmes.
  • The campus has a specific emphasis on the fields of Interactive Media, Game Art & Design and Digital Animation Production.

LIT Clonmel

Programme Offerings

The following programmes are offered in LIT Clonmel:
  • B.Sc. (Honours) in Creative Media & Design
  • B.Sc. (Ordinary) in Creative Media & Design
  • B.Sc. (Honours) in Digital Animation Production (Level 8)
  • B.Sc. (Honours) in Game Art & Design (Level 8)
  • B.Sc. (Honours) in Visual Effects (Level 8) (TBC for 2019)
  • All programme have embedded level 6 & 7 exit awards
  • Level 6 & 7 graduates can apply for level 8 add on awards

Why B.Sc. Programmes?

  • All programmes offered by LSAD at LIT Clonmel are Bachelor of Science degree programmes
  • Because of the amount of technological skills developed (including coding skills), the programmes are designated as Bachelor of Science programmes
  • No prior knowledge of technology is assumed
  • Two of the programmes are unique in being the only B.Sc. programmes in Ireland in the Game Art and Animation fields






Why a Portfolio?


  • In addition to academic ability (Leaving Cert or FETA), a portfolio allows applicants to demonstrate a creative capability, which is beneficial for a career in Creative Media, Animation and Game Art and Design






Digital Animation Production

  • Digital Animation Production took it's first intake of students in 2012.
  • Programme fully updated in 2017 based on stakeholder feedback.
  • Offered as a 4 Year B.Sc. (Honours) Degree in 2019
  • Developed in close consultation with the Animation industry
  • Targeting the 2D & 3D animation and visual effects sector
  • Includes a 3 month work placement at the end of year 3

Digital Animation Production

Entry Requirements
(Leaving Certificate)

  • Leaving Certificate applicants must attain a minimum of 2 H5 & 4 O6/H7 grades in six Leaving Certificate subjects, including Mathematics and English or Irish.
  • Applicants must also submit a portfolio of selected work for assessment

Digital Animation Production

Entry Requirements (QQI FETA)

  • QQI FETA (formerly FETAC) applicants must attain a full FETE Level 5 or Level 6 award, including a distinction grade in at least three modules
  • Applicants must also submit a portfolio of selected work for assessment

Game Art & Design

  • Game Art & Design is the latest offering at LSAD Clonmel
  • Offered as a 4 Year B.Sc. (Honours) Degree
  • Developed in consultation the Games Industry both in Ireland and Internationally.
  • Fully updated in 2017 based on stakeholder feedback.
  • Programme is focussed on the game content creation sector and working with industry leading content creations tools, scripting languages and games engines.
  • Career potential also outside core games sector into wider entertainment, advertising, and education sectors.
  • Includes a 3 month work placement at the end of year 3

Game Art & Design

Core Programme Elements

  • Game Design
  • Game Level Design
  • Drawing, Animation, Concept Art & 3D Modelling & Sculpting
  • Game Engines & Technologies including Virtual Reality
  • Game Environment Design including texturing and lighting for games
  • Personal & Professional Development

Portfolio

Portfolio Entry Requirements

  • Portfolio assessment take place around mid March
  • For Digital Animation Production & Game Art & Design, up to 600 points are allocated for portfolio and up to 600 for leaving certificate. Minimum of 240 points must be attained in the portfolio assessment element to successfully gain entry to the course.
  • For CMD, up to 200 points are allocated for portfolio (optional) and up to 600 for leaving certificate. No minimum entry on the portfolio is required for entry to the Creative Media & Design course.
